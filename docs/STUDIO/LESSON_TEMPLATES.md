## Purpose

Lesson templates provide recommended learning structures that help teachers create complete experiences efficiently. They reduce planning time, offer a reliable starting point, and promote pedagogical consistency across lessons.

Templates are optional. Teachers may adapt their lessons according to instructional goals, learner level, teaching context, and professional judgment. The template experience described here is **Future** and should not be represented as a current Studio capability until the canonical project documentation confirms it.

## Design Philosophy

A lesson template is not a fixed sequence. It is a reusable teaching blueprint based on PronounceLab's educational principles and common instructional needs.

Templates should guide teachers without limiting creativity. They suggest a coherent path, remind teachers of important learning stages, and make strong lesson planning easier. Teachers remain responsible for deciding which activities are necessary, how much practice learners need, and when a different sequence would work better.

## General Lesson Structure

PronounceLab lessons generally move from initial understanding toward independent use and reflection:

```text
Introduction
↓
Input
↓
Guided Practice
↓
Independent Practice
↓
Communication
↓
Reflection
```

- **Introduction** establishes the purpose and prepares learners to engage.
- **Input** provides language, examples, models, or context to observe and understand.
- **Guided Practice** supports learners while they begin using the target language.
- **Independent Practice** reduces support and asks learners to make more decisions.
- **Communication** gives learners a meaningful reason to use English.
- **Reflection** helps learners recognize progress and identify a useful next step.

Not every lesson must include every stage. A short lesson may combine stages, while a more demanding goal may require repetition or additional support. The learning objective should determine the structure.

## Official Lesson Templates

The following templates define the intended PronounceLab blueprint library. Recommended activities refer to the product direction described in the [Activity System](ACTIVITY_SYSTEM.md); some are not current Studio activity types.

### Pronunciation Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Help students recognize, produce, and apply a pronunciation pattern in meaningful speech.

**Recommended Activities:** Theory, Listening, Pronunciation, Interactive Practice, and AI Speaking Mission.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Theory
↓
Listening
↓
Pronunciation
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
AI Speaking Mission
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Begin with a clear sound pattern and useful examples. Let students hear the target before expecting production, then move from recognition and controlled repetition toward communication. For beginners, use words, spelling patterns, comparisons, and repeated exposure rather than relying on International Phonetic Alphabet symbols.

### Vocabulary Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Introduce useful vocabulary in context and help students apply it in communication.

**Recommended Activities:** Theory, Vocabulary, optional Listening, Interactive Practice, and an optional AI Speaking Mission.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Theory
↓
Vocabulary
↓
Listening (optional)
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
AI Speaking Mission (optional)
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Select a focused set of useful words or phrases connected by a situation or communicative goal. Avoid long isolated lists. Give learners repeated opportunities to understand, retrieve, and use the language.

### Grammar in Context Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Help students understand and use a grammar pattern as a tool for expressing meaning.

**Recommended Activities:** Theory, Grammar in Context, Interactive Practice, and AI Speaking Mission.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Theory
↓
Grammar in Context
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
AI Speaking Mission
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Begin with what the pattern helps learners communicate. Use clear examples in context, keep explanations proportionate, and move quickly toward supported and independent use. The aim is communication, not memorization of terminology.

### Listening Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Develop comprehension by helping students listen with a clear purpose and useful strategies.

**Recommended Activities:** Listening, Interactive Practice, and Reflection.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Listening
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
Reflection
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Prepare learners with a purpose before the audio begins. Allow more than one listen when useful, with a different focus each time. Practice should strengthen comprehension rather than test minor details without educational value.

### Reading Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Develop reading strategies and comprehension while reinforcing language in context.

**Recommended Activities:** Reading Comprehension, optional Vocabulary, Interactive Practice, and Reflection.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Reading Comprehension
↓
Vocabulary (optional)
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
Reflection
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Choose a text appropriate to the learner's level and give a clear reason to read. Move from general meaning toward relevant detail. Vocabulary work should support comprehension and future use instead of interrupting the text for every unfamiliar word.

### Speaking Lesson

**Educational Purpose:** Help students communicate meaning with growing fluency, confidence, and independence.

**Recommended Activities:** Optional Theory, Interactive Practice, AI Speaking Mission, and Reflection.

**Typical Learning Flow:**

```text
Theory (optional)
↓
Interactive Practice
↓
AI Speaking Mission
↓
Reflection
```

**Notes for Teachers:** Keep preparation connected to the speaking goal and leave enough time for actual communication. The AI Speaking Mission can extend practice and encourage independent effort, but it does not replace authentic classroom interaction, teacher feedback, or communication with other people.

## Combining Templates

Teachers may combine templates within the same lesson or across a unit when the learning objective benefits from more than one instructional focus. Useful combinations may include:

- Pronunciation and Vocabulary;
- Reading and Grammar in Context;
- Listening and Speaking.

Combination should remain purposeful. One template may provide the main structure while selected stages from another add necessary support. Teachers should avoid joining complete templates mechanically, as this can create a long or repetitive lesson. Flexibility is valuable when the learning objective remains clear.

## Choosing a Template

Start with the learning objective. Define what students should understand, practise, or communicate by the end of the lesson, then choose the template that best supports that outcome.

Do not select a template only because certain activities are available or familiar. If no template fits the objective well, adapt the closest blueprint or design a focused sequence using the same pedagogical principles. The template serves the lesson; the lesson does not serve the template.

## Future Templates

Additional templates may be introduced as common instructional needs become clear. Every new template should:

- solve a common instructional need;
- be reusable across suitable topics and levels;
- remain simple enough for teachers to understand quickly;
- align with PronounceLab pedagogy.

A new activity combination alone does not require a new template. The blueprint should provide a distinct and useful planning pattern that teachers are likely to use repeatedly.

## Conclusion

Lesson templates help teachers spend less time organizing lessons and more time creating meaningful learning experiences. They provide a confident starting point while preserving the freedom to respond to learners, context, and professional judgment.

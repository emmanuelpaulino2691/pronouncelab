## Purpose

This roadmap captures the long-term product vision for PronounceLab Studio and its authoring experience. It helps the team connect future decisions with the Studio's teacher-first mission, pedagogical principles, and commitment to simplicity.

The roadmap provides direction rather than a fixed development schedule. It does not assign release dates, delivery commitments, or implementation status. Priorities may change as teachers, students, educational practice, and the product evolve. Current delivery priorities and implemented behavior remain documented in the canonical [Project Roadmap](../ROADMAP.md) and project knowledge base.

## Roadmap Philosophy

PronounceLab grows through continuous improvement. Each step should solve a meaningful problem, strengthen the learning experience, or make good authoring easier for teachers.

New features should be added only when they provide clear educational value and preserve the simplicity of the Studio. Growth should reduce friction, clarify decisions, and expand useful teaching possibilities without creating an overloaded product.

The goal is not to have the most features. It is to provide the best teaching experience: focused, dependable, creative, and easy to understand.

## Current Foundation

The Studio Handbook establishes a shared product foundation for future authoring decisions:

- **Courses** define broad learning journeys.
- **Units** organize those journeys into coherent stages.
- **Lessons** provide focused learning experiences.
- **Activities** define what students are expected to do.
- **Blocks** provide reusable content that supports each activity.
- **Lesson Templates** offer optional blueprints for effective learning flows.
- **Validation** guides teachers toward complete, publication-ready experiences.
- **Teacher-first workflow** keeps educational intent ahead of software administration.

Together, these concepts describe a consistent path from curriculum planning to meaningful student practice. They also provide the language needed to evaluate future Studio improvements.

## Short-Term Vision

The short-term direction focuses on making core authoring work clearer, faster, and more confident.

### Richer Activity Authoring

Teachers should receive focused authoring experiences that match the educational purpose of each activity. Improvements should help them create useful content without exposing unnecessary choices.

### Reusable Block Library

A clear block library should make common instructions, media, examples, practice, and assessment content easier to create and reuse. The library should improve consistency while remaining simple to navigate.

### Improved Lesson Preview

Teachers should be able to experience a lesson from the student's perspective before publication. Preview should support review of clarity, sequence, pacing, accessibility, and the complete learning journey.

### Faster Authoring Workflow

Common tasks should require less repetition and fewer decisions. Templates, sensible starting points, duplication, and clear next steps should reduce planning effort without removing teacher control.

### Better Media Management

Teachers should be able to find, understand, and use educational media with confidence. Media workflows should keep attention on instructional purpose, suitability, and the learner experience.

### Stronger Accessibility

Accessibility should shape authoring guidance, content choices, preview, and review. Teachers should receive practical support for creating experiences that more learners can perceive, understand, and complete.

## Medium-Term Vision

The medium-term direction expands reuse, guidance, and collaboration across the Studio. These areas are product goals, not delivery promises.

### AI-Assisted Lesson Creation

AI assistance may help teachers develop starting points, examples, practice ideas, and lesson variations. It should remain transparent, editable, and guided by the teacher. Assistance should save time without replacing professional judgment.

### Reusable Lesson Templates

Teachers should be able to begin with trusted pedagogical blueprints and adapt them to their objectives. Templates should promote complete learning flows while preserving creativity.

### Shared Content Libraries

Useful blocks, activities, examples, and lesson patterns may be shared for responsible reuse. Libraries should help teachers build on proven material without encouraging thoughtless copying or content overload.

### Smarter Recommendations

The Studio may suggest relevant activities, blocks, templates, or review actions based on the teacher's learning objective and authoring context. Recommendations should explain their educational value and remain optional.

### Collaborative Authoring

Teachers and content teams may work together through clear review, feedback, and approval experiences. Collaboration should strengthen quality and shared understanding without turning authoring into unnecessary administration.

### Richer Assessment Experiences

Assessment should provide useful evidence of understanding through varied, purposeful practice. New experiences should support learning and feedback rather than add testing for its own sake.

## Long-Term Vision

The long-term ambition is a Studio that helps teachers design responsive, communication-focused English learning at greater depth and scale.

### Adaptive Learning Experiences

Lessons may respond more thoughtfully to learner needs, readiness, and progress while preserving clear educational goals and teacher oversight.

### Intelligent Authoring Assistance

Authoring support may recognize gaps, suggest coherent learning progressions, and help teachers refine content. Intelligence should make decisions easier to understand, not hide educational reasoning.

### Personalized Learning Paths

Teachers may design or guide learning paths that respond to different goals, levels, and areas for improvement. Personalization should remain truthful, manageable, and connected to meaningful evidence.

### Expanded Communication Activities

The Studio may support a broader range of speaking, listening, reading, and collaborative communication experiences. Expansion should prioritize authentic language use over novelty.

### Broader English Teaching Contexts

PronounceLab may support more learner levels, course purposes, teaching settings, and forms of English communication while maintaining a coherent product identity.

Simplicity must remain a core value throughout this evolution. More capable should not mean more confusing. The Studio should continue to feel calm, focused, and teacher-centered even as its possibilities grow.

## Product Decision Criteria

Every future roadmap item should be tested against the following questions:

- Does this improve teaching?
- Does this improve learning?
- Does this simplify authoring or carefully contain new complexity?
- Does this align with PronounceLab philosophy?
- Does it justify its complexity?

A promising idea may still need refinement, postponement, or rejection if its educational value is unclear or its cost to simplicity is too high.

## Living Roadmap

This roadmap is expected to evolve continuously. Items may be added, removed, reordered, combined, or refined as the Studio matures and better evidence becomes available.

The roadmap reflects direction rather than guarantees. Its horizons describe relative product thinking, not fixed dates or committed sequence. Any change should remain consistent with the [Studio Vision](STUDIO_VISION.md), [Pedagogical Principles](PEDAGOGICAL_PRINCIPLES.md), and current canonical project documentation.

## Conclusion

This roadmap exists to ensure that PronounceLab Studio evolves intentionally. Every stage of growth should remain guided by educational value, teacher experience, and the belief that a simpler product can support better teaching.
